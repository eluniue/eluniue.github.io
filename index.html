What is AquaCaptor?

AquaCaptor is a concept-level atmospheric water harvesting project that explores MOF/Salt composite adsorbents to capture moisture from air under low relative humidity.

Why it matters

The project targets decentralized, low-energy water production for water-stressed regions where conventional infrastructure is limited.

Project Vision

AquaCaptor aims to contribute to sustainable water access by developing an atmospheric water capture concept based on MOF/Salt composite materials.

Project Mission

The mission of AquaCaptor is to investigate material-based atmospheric water harvesting mechanisms and propose an engineering-oriented prototype.

Problem

Freshwater scarcity and uneven water distribution create a need for decentralized and low-energy water harvesting solutions.

Proposed Solution

AquaCaptor utilizes MOF/Salt composite adsorbents to capture atmospheric moisture under low relative humidity conditions.

Prototype Consept

The prototype consists of a modular adsorption unit containing MOF/Salt composite materials with passive or low-energy desorption.

Project Outcomes

  • Conceptual design of a MOF/Salt-based atmospheric water harvesting system
  • Justification of material selection based on adsorption and desorption characteristics
  • Evaluation of the feasibility of low-energy atmospheric water capture
  • Preliminary assessment of potential applications in water-stressed regions
